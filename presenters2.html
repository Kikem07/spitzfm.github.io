Foundational Harlem & Bronx Twin Turntables

DJ Rhythm Shay

Before hip‑hop had a name, DJ Rhythm Shay was laying its foundation in smoky Harlem basements and Bronx block parties. Blending jump blues, doo‑wop, and early rock ’n’ roll with rhythmic spoken word, Shay used twin turntables to loop breaks from R&B records and hype the crowd with rhymed shout‑outs. Her sets were raw, electric, and infectious — often featuring live percussion and dancers freestyling to her beats.

Though the term “hip‑hop” wouldn’t emerge until decades later, Shay’s rhythmic innovations and crowd‑moving charisma made her a legend among street DJs. She’s remembered as one of the earliest architects of the culture, spinning vinyl and spitting rhymes before the world knew what to call it.
